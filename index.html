What is S-Designer?

"Low code, no code" (LCNC) refers to a modern approach to software development that emphasizes the use of visual interfaces, drag-and-drop tools, and pre-built components to create applications and workflows with minimal or no traditional coding required.

ADD TAB
1.

Visual Development Environment:

LCNC platforms provide intuitive, drag-and-drop interfaces that enable users to design and build applications visually, without writing code.
2.

Pre-built Components and Templates:

LCNC platforms offer a library of pre-built components, templates, and integrations that users can easily incorporate into their applications or workflows.
3.

Workflow Automation:

Users can automate repetitive tasks and streamline complex workflows by visually designing sequences of actions and triggers.
4.

Empowerment of Citizen Developers:

LCNC platforms democratize software development by enabling individuals with domain expertise, but limited coding experience, to create custom solutions tailored to their specific needs.
5.

Rapid Prototyping and Iteration:

LCNC empowers users to quickly prototype ideas, experiment with different features, and iterate based on feedback, accelerating the development process.

Our development methodology is based on two approaches,

"User-centric"
"Total-Cost-of-Ownership (TCO) - centric"

User-centric approach of Small Planet exactly revolves around this recent concept - "how the User is going to feel about our software". Note, this approach is One Step Ahead of customer-centric approach (i.e. customer satisfaction or customer delight). The End Users of a software are one step Further Down the Line.

TCO-centric approach, affects the entire development and maintenance cycle. We need to design the software in such a way that the initial cost of development remains under control and Also the future Several Years of Maintenance and Service cost remains affordable.
